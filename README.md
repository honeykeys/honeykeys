# Karl Nuyda

Notes to myself about working with machines that almost think.

I love working with models. The magic wore off around month three, which is about when I started actually loving them. They know too much and remember too little. Their attention wanders. They make things up. They're overconfident and sycophantic in the same breath. They hold most of what people have ever written and still need to be told how to use it. They remind me of someone.

So the work, for me, is not getting them to say more. It's getting them to say only what holds up. Build the checking in. Anchor to something true. Keep a human in the seat. A model can't judge its own work anyway. It can't see past its own output. So nothing I build trusts itself, and that turns out to be the whole design.

I came to code through English Literature, which sounds like a detour and wasn't. Literate programming was the bridge: a program is prose that also happens to run. I like building things that read like they mean it. Hard in the structure, soft in the hue. Honest when someone's checking. Honest when no one is.

### Things I'm making

- **resume-loop**: honesty as an engineering problem. Three models read blind, every claim checked against a fact base. Public on purpose.
- **tingin**: teaching a machine the handoff between nurses.
- **hargow**: a classroom that pays attention back.
- **kyb-pdf-parser**: reading meaning out of bank statements.
- **ferrokit**: the starter kit I reach for.
